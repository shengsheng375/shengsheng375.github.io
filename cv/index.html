Work

  • 2025.2 - Present
    Undergraduate Research Assistant
    Cardiovascular MRI Group @ Northwestern Unversity Feinberg School of Medicine
    Developed and optimized a cross-institutional, two-stage deep-learning workflow for thoracic aorta segmentation in computed tomography angiography scans (CTA), trained on over 380 paired scans from German and U.S. centers—achieving median Dice scores of ~0.95 on same-site data and over 0.93 when combining both datasets, dramatically cutting manual segmentation time. Facilitated an automated preprocessing pipeline that uses lung detection to crop and resample CTA volumes, standardizing inputs for the segmentation model.
  • 2023.5 - 2023.10
    Research Intern
    Functional Optical Imaging Laboratory @ Northwestern Unversity, Department of Biomedical Engineering
    Succeeded advancing diagnostic imaging for glaucoma through developing a robotic system for anterior segment optical coherence tomography (OCT). Created a feature-based montaging algorithm addressing robotic positioning inaccuracies, facilitating precise alignment of multiple OCT scans without reliance on robotic coordinates alone. Improved visualization of ocular aqueous humor outflow pathways essential for glaucoma management.
  • 2021.8 - 2022.9
    Research Assistant
    Functional Optical Imaging Laboratory @ Northwestern Unversity, Department of Biomedical Engineering
    Assisted in the development of SpeNet, a spectral classification neural network aimed to improve imaging accuracy in spectroscopic single-molecule localization microscopy (sSMLM) for visualizing low-abundance protein interactions. Drove molecular misidentification down to ≤1.7% (0.5% with score cut-off); a 3 to 1l times improvement over conventional spectral-filtering. Devised an image segmentation model under Dr. Benjamin Brenner, analyzing outer membrane protein distribution using the TOMM20 marker. Produced a supervised learning model to distinguish between adjacent mitochondrial structures in super-resolution images.
